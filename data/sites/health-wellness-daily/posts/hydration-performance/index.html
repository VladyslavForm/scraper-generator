Hydration and Athletic Performance

By Dr. Kevin Anderson, PhD | Published: January 17, 2025

Proper hydration is fundamental to athletic performance and overall health. Even mild dehydration impairs physical and cognitive function, making hydration strategy crucial for anyone engaging in regular exercise.

Why Hydration Matters

Water regulates body temperature, transports nutrients, removes waste, and lubricates joints. During exercise, fluid loss through sweat increases dramatically, potentially compromising performance and health if not replaced adequately.

Performance Impact

Research shows that losing just 2% of body weight through fluid loss can decrease performance by 10-20% in many activities.

Daily Hydration Needs

Individual requirements vary based on body size, activity level, climate, and sweat rate. A general guideline is 30-40ml per kilogram of body weight daily, increasing with exercise intensity and duration.

Exercise Hydration Strategy

Begin exercise well-hydrated by consuming 5-7ml per kilogram body weight 2-4 hours before. During exercise lasting over 60 minutes, drink 150-250ml every 15-20 minutes. Post-exercise, replace 150% of fluid lost to account for ongoing losses.

Electrolyte Considerations

For exercise exceeding 60 minutes or in hot conditions, electrolyte replacement becomes important. Sodium is the primary electrolyte lost in sweat and should be replaced through sports drinks or salted foods during extended activity.

Conclusion

Optimizing hydration enhances performance, recovery, and health. Monitor urine color, track body weight changes, and develop a personalized hydration strategy based on your individual sweat rate and exercise conditions.
