London's Museums: A Journey Through Time

By James Harrison

Published: October 10, 2025

England

London's world-class museums offer journeys through human history, art, and achievement. Best of all, the major ones are free, making culture accessible to everyone.

The British Museum

Housing treasures from every corner of the ancient world, the British Museum is overwhelming in scope. I focused on a few key areas: the Egyptian galleries with the Rosetta Stone, the Greek sculptures from the Parthenon, and the Assyrian lion hunt reliefs. Even after four hours, I'd seen perhaps a tenth of the collection.

The Tate Modern

Housed in a former power station, the Tate Modern makes contemporary art accessible and exciting. I spent an afternoon with works by Warhol, Hockney, and Hirst. The view from the top floor across the Thames to St. Paul's Cathedral is worth the visit alone.

The Natural History Museum

The dinosaur galleries transported me back to childhood wonder. The architecture of the museum itself—Victorian Gothic at its finest—rivals the exhibits inside.

London's museums are democratic institutions where knowledge and beauty belong to everyone. Take advantage of this generosity.
