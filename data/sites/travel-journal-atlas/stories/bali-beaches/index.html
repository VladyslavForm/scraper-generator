Bali's Hidden Beaches: Beyond the Tourist Trail

By Maya Anderson

Published: October 24, 2025

Indonesia

While tourists flock to Kuta and Seminyak, Bali's best beaches hide in plain sight, waiting for those willing to venture beyond the well-trodden path. I spent two weeks discovering secret coves, pristine shores, and local beach communities that few guidebooks mention.

Green Bowl Beach: The Hidden Gem

Accessible only by descending 300 steep steps carved into the limestone cliff, Green Bowl Beach rewards the effort with powdery white sand and turquoise waters. At low tide, sea caves reveal themselves, perfect for exploration. I was one of only five people on this spectacular beach.

Bias Tugel: The Secret Beach

Near Padang Bai, a narrow path winds through a village and down to Bias Tugel, a crescent of white sand framed by palm trees. The locals who manage the beach maintain its pristine condition. I spent hours swimming, reading, and enjoying fresh coconuts from a small warung.

Nyang Nyang Beach: Untouched Paradise

This three-kilometer stretch of empty beach sits beneath towering cliffs. The walk down takes 20 minutes through scrubby terrain, but you'll likely have the entire beach to yourself. The sunset here is among the best I've seen in Bali.
