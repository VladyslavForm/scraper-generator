Amsterdam by Canal: A Water-Level View

By Anna van der Berg

Published: October 2, 2025

Netherlands

Amsterdam is defined by its canals—UNESCO lists them as a World Heritage site. Seeing the city from water level reveals its true character and history.

Canal Belt History

The concentric canal belt was planned in the 17th century during the Dutch Golden Age. I took a small boat tour that explained how Amsterdam's merchants built tall, narrow houses along the canals. The distinctive gabled roofs and canal houses create Amsterdam's signature look.

Houseboat Life

Over 2,500 houseboats line Amsterdam's canals. I visited the Houseboat Museum to understand this unique lifestyle. Living on water comes with challenges—plumbing, electricity, and the constant movement—but residents wouldn't trade it for anything.

Cycling the Canals

I rented a bike and cycled along the canal paths, the Amsterdam way of life. The city is perfectly flat, bike lanes are everywhere, and locals navigate with remarkable skill. I stopped at canal-side cafés, crossed endless bridges, and discovered that the best views come from moving slowly.

Amsterdam's canals are not just pretty—they're the city's foundation, its transportation network, and its soul.
