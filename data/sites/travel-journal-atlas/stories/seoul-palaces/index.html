Seoul's Five Grand Palaces: Royal Heritage

By Ji-Woo Park

Published: October 4, 2025

South Korea

Seoul's five grand palaces offer windows into Korea's royal past, when the Joseon Dynasty ruled for over 500 years. These architectural treasures survived wars and occupation to remain symbols of Korean identity.

Gyeongbokgung: The Main Palace

The largest and most famous palace, Gyeongbokgung was built in 1395. I timed my visit for the changing of the guard ceremony, where performers in traditional costumes reenact historical protocols. The throne hall and the private quarters reveal the formality and hierarchy of court life.

Changdeokgung and the Secret Garden

This palace is known for its beautiful Secret Garden, a landscaped area where royalty sought refuge from court politics. The garden follows Korean principles of harmony with nature—buildings nestle into the landscape rather than dominating it.

Palace Life and History

A guide explained how the palaces functioned: the outer courts for official business, the inner courts for royal family living, and the gardens for contemplation. The Japanese occupation destroyed many buildings, making reconstruction efforts both architectural and political statements.

Seoul's palaces are not just historical sites—they're living monuments to Korean resilience and cultural pride.
