Chiang Mai's Temples: Northern Thailand's Spiritual Heart

By Tom Richards

Published: September 26, 2025

Thailand

Chiang Mai's 300+ Buddhist temples range from ancient ruins to glittering golden stupas. Temple hopping here offers insight into Northern Thai culture and Buddhism.

Wat Phra That Doi Suthep

Perched on a mountain overlooking Chiang Mai, Doi Suthep is Northern Thailand's most sacred temple. I climbed the 306-step staircase (flanked by naga serpent railings) to reach the golden chedi. Monks chanted evening prayers as the sun set over the city below—a moment of peace and reflection.

Wat Chedi Luang

This ancient temple in the old city features a massive partially-ruined chedi from the 14th century. An earthquake damaged it in 1545, but the ruins remain impressive. I attended a monk chat program where English-speaking monks answer questions about Buddhism and Thai culture.

Temple Etiquette

I learned the rules: remove shoes before entering, dress modestly (cover shoulders and knees), speak quietly, don't point feet at Buddha images, and request permission before photographing monks. Most temples welcome visitors who show respect.

Chiang Mai's temples are not museums—they're active places of worship where locals come daily to make offerings, meditate, and seek blessings. Visit with respect and openness, and you'll gain insight into Thai spiritual life.
