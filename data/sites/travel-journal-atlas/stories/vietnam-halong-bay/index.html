Halong Bay: Vietnam's Emerald Waters

By Linh Nguyen

Published: October 12, 2025

Vietnam

Halong Bay's limestone karsts rising from emerald waters create one of the world's most recognizable landscapes. I spent three days sailing through this UNESCO World Heritage site.

Cruising the Bay

I joined a small junk boat with just eight cabins, avoiding the massive cruise ships. We sailed through thousands of limestone islands, each more photogenic than the last. The captain navigated to quieter areas where we seemed to have this natural wonder to ourselves.

Kayaking Through Caves

We kayaked through a sea cave that opened into a hidden lagoon, completely surrounded by sheer cliffs. Monkeys watched from above as we floated in serene silence. This felt like discovery, even though countless others have made the same journey.

Floating Villages

We visited a floating fishing village where families have lived for generations. Children rowed small boats with impressive skill, and fishermen demonstrated pearl farming. It's a way of life gradually disappearing as tourism reshapes the bay.
